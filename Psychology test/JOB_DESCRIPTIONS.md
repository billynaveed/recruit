# Job Descriptions — YFS Test Roles

---

## 1. Chief Executive Officer

**Organisation:** Young Founders School  
**Location:** Singapore (hybrid, with regular travel across APAC)  
**Reports to:** Board of Directors  
**Type:** Full-time

---

### About Young Founders School

Young Founders School (YFS) is an educational nonprofit on a mission to unlock entrepreneurial potential in students across Asia. Over the past decade we have run programmes for thousands of young people, partnering with major technology companies, financial institutions, and universities. We are now entering our next chapter: scaling our impact tenfold, launching new AI-native curricula, and building YFS into the defining entrepreneurship platform for the next generation of Asian founders.

---

### The Role

We are looking for a Chief Executive Officer to lead YFS through this transformation. You will own the strategy, the team, the fundraising, and the public face of the organisation. You will inherit a strong brand, a loyal corporate partner base, and a small but capable team, and you will be expected to roughly triple our reach within three years while keeping the quality of our programmes uncompromised.

This is not a ceremonial CEO role. You will be hands-on. You will write fundraising decks yourself, sit in on curriculum design, personally close partnership deals, and spend real time with students. You will also be the person who decides what YFS does next, and you will be held accountable for whether it works.

---

### What You Will Do

- Set the three-year strategy and own the annual operating plan
- Lead all fundraising activity: grants, corporate partnerships, individual donors, and any new revenue lines you design
- Build, coach, and retain a team of eight to fifteen people across programmes, partnerships, and operations
- Own the P&L and report quarterly to the Board
- Represent YFS externally: speaking, writing, media, partner conversations
- Oversee curriculum direction, including the integration of AI tools into every programme
- Make the calls on what programmes to launch, continue, and shut down

---

### What We Are Looking For

- A track record of running something. This could be a company, a programme, a team, or a meaningful initiative. We care that you have shipped outcomes, not that you have held a specific title.
- Strong commercial instincts. You understand fundraising, partnerships, and how to make the numbers work.
- A genuine interest in young people, education, and entrepreneurship in Asia.
- Evidence of high agency. You do not wait for permission. When you see a problem, you fix it or you delegate it; you do not sit on it.
- Fluency with modern tools, particularly AI. We expect the CEO to be personally using AI daily and to set the bar for the rest of the team.
- Comfort with ambiguity. Much of your first year will involve figuring out what YFS should become, not just executing a plan someone else wrote.
- Willingness to travel across Hong Kong, Singapore, India, and wider APAC.

---

### Nice to Have

- Prior experience scaling a nonprofit or mission-driven business
- An existing network among Asian corporates, family offices, or philanthropic foundations
- Background in education, technology, or finance
- Written or spoken Mandarin or Cantonese

---

### Role-Specific Application Questions (10)

1. What does excellent performance in the CEO role look like in the first 90 days, and how would you deliver it?
2. Tell us about a time you had to solve an ambiguous problem that is similar to the challenges in this CEO role. What did you do, and what changed because of your work?
3. Describe the strongest proof of work you would point to for this CEO application, and explain why it matters.
4. If you joined YFS in this CEO role next month, what would you stop, start, and improve first?
5. Describe a project where you had to earn trust across stakeholders with different priorities. How did you do it?
6. What trade-offs would you make when speed, quality, and resource constraints collide in this kind of role?
7. Tell us about a time you improved a broken process. What was wrong, what changed, and how did you measure the improvement?
8. What parts of this CEO role play to your strengths, and where would you need to learn quickly?
9. Describe a moment when you showed unusual ownership or agency without being asked. What happened?
10. Running the whole organisation, describe how you would approach the most important challenge in this role.

## 2. Marketing Engineer

**Organisation:** Young Founders School  
**Location:** London (Hybrid)  
**Department:** Marketing  
**Type:** Full-time  
**Level:** Graduate

---

### The Role

Young Founders School is looking for a Marketing Engineer who sits at the intersection of creativity and technology. This is a graduate-level role for someone who:

- Has a genuine creative streak — they make things, not just talk about making things
- Wields AI tools fluently and is already using them in their own work and life
- Has built at least some basic automations — Zapier, Make, scripts, or anything that shows they enjoy making systems that do work for them
- Has a strong learning orientation and can point to things they have taught themselves outside of school

This is not a traditional marketing role. We are not looking for someone who has run social media campaigns and knows what CPM means. We are looking for someone who is curious, technical enough to execute, creative enough to generate ideas that work, and genuinely excited about what AI is doing to the craft of marketing.

You will work directly with the leadership team on content, campaigns, and the growth of YFS's reach. You will have real ownership from day one.

---

### What We Are Really Looking For

- **Creative output**: Can you point to things you have actually made — content, projects, experiments — that show taste and initiative?
- **AI fluency**: Not just awareness. Are you actively using AI tools? Have you built with them?
- **Builder instinct**: Have you ever set up an automation, written a script, or built a system — even a small one — to make your own life more efficient?
- **Self-directed learning**: Can you show us something you taught yourself because you wanted to, not because it was on a syllabus?
- **Genuine curiosity about YFS's mission**: This is an education organisation for young founders. Do you care about that?

---

### Role-Specific Application Questions (5)

1. Describe a creative project you have worked on — personal, academic, or professional — that you are genuinely proud of. What made it stand out and what did you build or create?
2. Tell us about an AI tool, automation, or script you have built or used in your own life or studies. What did it do, how did you build or set it up, and what problem did it solve?
3. How do you think AI is changing marketing over the next two to three years? How are you personally preparing for that shift, and what have you already experimented with?
4. If you had to launch a marketing campaign for YFS with a £500 budget, one week of your own time, and no team, what would you actually do? Be as specific as possible.
5. Describe something you have taught yourself outside of school or university in the last two years. Why did you pursue it, how did you go about it, and what did you produce or achieve as a result?

## Standard Questions (Both Roles)

All candidates answer these 6 questions regardless of role:

1. Tell us about a time you identified a problem no one else had noticed and took initiative to solve it. What did you do and what happened?
2. Describe a project or goal you worked on that did not go as planned. How did you respond and what did you learn?
3. What is one skill or area of knowledge you have taught yourself in the past two years? Why did you pursue it and how did you go about it?
4. Describe a situation where you disagreed with someone more senior than you. How did you handle it and what was the outcome?
5. What is the most ambitious thing you have attempted, regardless of whether it succeeded? What drove you to try it?
6. Where do you want to be in five years, and how does this role connect to that path?

## Psychometric Assessment (Both Roles)

All candidates complete the same 22-item core assessment:

- **4 STAR behavioural items** (C-S1–C-S4): open-ended written responses, minimum 150 characters each, AI rubric-scored
- **13 forced-choice items** (C-F1–C-F13): paired trait comparisons measuring conscientiousness, honesty/humility, composure, learning orientation, interpersonal style, and motivational drivers
- **1 tradeoff rank** (C-T1): rank 4 motivational drivers (autonomy, mission, scope, stability)
- **1 tradeoff choice** (C-T2): integrity scenario with 4 response options, scored for honesty/humility
- **2 consistency checks** (C-CC1, C-CC2): Likert self-assessments cross-referenced against forced-choice patterns
- **1 reflection** (C-R1): optional open-ended, unscored
